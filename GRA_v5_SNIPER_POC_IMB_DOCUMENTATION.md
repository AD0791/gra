### POC (Point of Control) Deep Dive

#### What Is POC?

The Point of Control is the price level where the **most volume** was traded within a single candle. Think of it as the "center of gravity" for that candle's activity.

#### Why POC Matters for Futures Scalping

1. **Institutional Footprint**: Big players don't move markets with single orders. They execute at optimal prices - the POC shows where they agreed.

2. **Support/Resistance Magnet**: Price tends to revisit POC levels because:
   - Unfilled orders may still exist there
   - Algorithms target these levels
   - Other traders anchor their analysis to volume

3. **Stop Loss Placement**: Place stops on the opposite side of POC for protection.

### Imbalances Deep Dive

#### What Are Imbalances?

Imbalances occur when buy volume **significantly exceeds** sell volume (or vice versa) at specific price levels within a candle. They represent:

- **Unfilled orders** that couldn't find counterparties
- **Aggressive institutional activity** overwhelming the other side
- **Future magnets** where price tends to return
